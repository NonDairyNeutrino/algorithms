        \underline{\textbf{Solution}}

        Let there be polynomials $A$ and $B$ of degree $n = 3$ defined by 

        \begin{subequations}
            \begin{equation}
                A(x) = 7 x^3 - x^2 + x - 10 \implies A = \langle -10, 1, -1, 7 \rangle
            \end{equation}
            \begin{equation}
                B(x) = 8x^3 - 6x + 3 \implies B = \langle 3, -6, 0, 8\rangle
